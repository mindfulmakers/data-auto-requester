1️⃣ **Consumer services that help retrieve or remove your data**

These tools automate requests to companies and data brokers on your behalf.

**Examples**

**Incogni**

- Sends automated privacy requests to hundreds of data brokers.
- Can request access or deletion of data tied to your email and identity.
- One of the most widely used consumer tools. 

**DeleteMe**

- Focuses on removing your information from “people-search” and broker sites.
- Performs recurring scans and removal requests.

**OneRep**

- Targets U.S. data brokers and people-finder websites.
- Sends opt-out and deletion requests automatically.

**Kanary / Optery**

- Similar services that monitor broker databases and request removals.

👉 These tools mainly deal with **data brokers and people-search sites**, not big platforms like Google or Apple.

 

2️⃣ **Apps that help you discover what companies hold your data**

These services scan your accounts and show which companies likely hold your data.

Examples:

**Mine (SayMine)**

- Connect your email.
- It scans receipts and account confirmations to identify companies holding your data.
- Lets you send deletion or access requests.

**PrivacyBee**

- Finds companies with your data.
- Sends automated opt-out or access requests.

These are useful for mapping **where your data exists**.

 

3️⃣ **Enterprise privacy tools (not really consumer products)**

These are built for companies to manage incoming data requests, but sometimes individuals use them indirectly.

Examples:

- **OneTrust**
- **DataGrail**
- **TrustArc**
- **Osano**

These platforms automate **Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs)** — the process companies use to respond when someone asks for their personal data. 

They are usually sold to corporations rather than individuals.

 

4️⃣ **The limitation of these tools**

Even the best consumer services **cannot retrieve everything**.

They generally cannot automatically obtain:

- Google Takeout data
- Apple Health exports
- Airline data
- Uber trip logs
- Healthcare records
- Credit bureau files

For those, you still have to **request them directly**.

 

5️⃣ **The best strategy (what privacy professionals do)**

Instead of relying on one service, people usually combine:

1. **Manual exports**
   - Google Takeout
   - Apple privacy portal
   - Amazon data request
   - Uber export
   - Experian consumer file

1. **Broker removal tools**

- Incogni
- DeleteMe
- OneRep

1. **Tracking spreadsheet**

- List each company
- Date requested
- Date received

 

🧠 **Reality check**

Right now there is **no single company that can retrieve all your data from every service**.

The reason:

- Each company requires **identity verification**
- Many exports must be triggered **inside your account**
- Laws require **individual consent per request**

So the process cannot be fully centralized.
